Key Problems with Traditional System

Extremely Slow

6-9 months from application to funding. Emergency needs can't wait that long.

High Overhead

15-25% of funds go to administration, not programs. Inefficient use of donor money.

No Community Voice

Decisions made by committees in capital cities. Beneficiaries have no say.

Poor Transparency

Internal reports only. Public can't see where money goes or impact achieved.

Rigid Requirements

Small organizations can't compete. Favors large INGOs with grant writers.

Short-term Cycles

1-3 year grants don't build sustainable systems. Constant reapplication burden.

Bypasses Local Systems

Creates parallel structures instead of strengthening government capacity.

Limited Accountability

Self-reported impact. No independent validation or real-time monitoring.

Real Example: COVID-19

When COVID-19 hit in early 2020:

  • Traditional grant cycles took 6-9 months
  • By the time funding arrived, first wave was over
  • Emergency response money came through donations, not grants
  • No system for rapid community-driven allocation
  • Parallel emergency systems created, not integrated with health systems

The Need for Innovation

Traditional grant systems were designed for:

  • Long-term development projects (not emergencies)
  • Large international NGOs (not local organizations)
  • Predictable contexts (not volatile situations)
  • Donor accountability (not beneficiary empowerment)

What's Needed Instead:

  • Speed - Weeks not months
  • Democracy - Community voice in allocation
  • Transparency - Real-time public tracking
  • Flexibility - Adapt to changing needs
  • Local Ownership - Build local capacity
  • Integration - Strengthen government systems
  • Innovation - Use AI, blockchain, mobile tech

This is Why We Built PGPX

PGPX combines the best of traditional grants (professional management, accountability) with modern innovations (QF democracy, DID reputation, AI monitoring, blockchain transparency).
