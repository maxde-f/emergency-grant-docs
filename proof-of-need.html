Why "Need" Is the Missing Primitive

The world has Proof of Work and Proof of Stake for blockchains, and Proof of Impact emerging in public-good finance. But the system lacks a primitive that answers the most essential moral question:

How do we know, verifiably and safely, where need is real, urgent, and significant?

Without this primitive, all other proofs risk misalignment. We measure what happened (impact) without understanding what should have happened (need).

The Core Problem

Funding decisions are often influenced by: donor preferences, historical commitments, advocacy strength, reporting structures, political optics, or legacy budgets. Meanwhile, the actual distribution of need among real people rarely determines how resources flow.

What Is Proof of Need?

Proof of Need (PoN) is an epistemic and ethical framework that defines how systems should recognize, validate, and prioritize human need before resources are allocated.

Not a data point. Not an "input." A source of epistemic legitimacy.

The Four Pillars of Proof of Need

S - Subjective Recognition

Lived experience as primary truth

Communities, activists, frontline workers, and affected people assert:

  • What is missing
  • What is fragile
  • What is dangerous
  • What threatens dignity or life

This subjective layer captures: stigma, safety risks, inaccessibility, exhaustion of services, bureaucratic barriers, psychological needs, declining trust, early indicators of collapse.

O - Objective Validation

Multiple independent evidence streams

  • Service utilization patterns
  • Workforce shortages
  • Epidemiological signals
  • Financial risks & supply-chain stress
  • Geospatial & market data
  • Governance disruptions

Objective data does not override subjective truth. It provides triangulation and denoising.

R - Continuity Risk

Risk of service collapse

  • Funding runway depletion
  • Single-provider fragility
  • Conflict intensity
  • Provider arrest/shutdown risk
  • Supply chain disruption

A system 20% likely to collapse is more than twice as dangerous as one 10% likely. Fragility multiplies need.

U - Structural Vulnerability

Latent injustice amplifiers

  • Criminalization of key populations
  • Intensity of stigma
  • Repression & human rights violations
  • Policy barriers
  • Informal costs of accessing care

This makes the system explicitly bias toward the most structurally exposed groups.

The Mathematical Formula

For a given community/region/project i, the Proof of Need score is:

PoNi = wS·Si + wO·Oi + wR·Ri + wU·Ui
Component Description Normalization
Si Subjective/community need (what people say) [0, 1]
Oi Objective indicators (what data shows) [0, 1]
Ri Continuity risk (how close to service collapse) [0, 1]
Ui Structural vulnerability (criminalization, conflict, etc.) [0, 1]

Constraints: wS + wO + wR + wU = 1, all weights ≥ 0

The Quadratic Trick for Community Voice

For the subjective component, we borrow from Quadratic Funding:

Si = (Σj √sij)²

Where sij ∈ [0,1] is the need intensity reported by person j in community i.

Why Square Roots?

  • Many moderate signals > few extreme ones — shared experience of moderate need = strong signal
  • Diminishing returns on individual intensity — no incentive to exaggerate
  • Symmetry with QF — shared structure makes mechanisms interoperable

From Need to Funding Allocation

Once we have PoNi for each community, we allocate from a fixed pool F:

Fundingi = F · (PoNi)κ / Σj(PoNj)κ

Where κ controls responsiveness to need:

  • κ = 1 → proportional allocation
  • κ > 1 → strongly favor high-need communities (convex)
  • κ < 1 → smoother distribution (concave)

Concrete Example

Two Communities: A and B

Weights: wS=0.3, wO=0.2, wR=0.3, wU=0.2

With κ=2 (strong pro-need bias):

ShareA = 0.126 / 0.595 ≈ 21.2%

Result

Community B receives ~79% of funding despite Community A having stronger community voice. This matches the philosophical intuition: continuity risk + structural vulnerability override raw volume of signals.

The Self-Correcting System

Need produces weight → Weight produces allocation → Allocation produces action → Action produces measured impact → Impact updates need → Need resets the next cycle's governance parameters.

Combining PoN with PoI

Proof of Need and Proof of Impact form a closed accountability loop:

Scorei = (PoNi)κ · (PoIi)λ

PoN asks:

  • What is required?
  • Who is at risk?
  • What is fragile?

κ — how much to prioritize need

PoI asks:

  • Did we deliver real value?
  • What changed in people's lives?
  • Was impact verified?

λ — how much to reward demonstrated impact

Evolution strategy:

  • Early-stage systems: set λ low (need first)
  • As data accumulates: increase λ gradually (need + performance)
  • The system evolves naturally rather than by governance fiat

The Ethics of Proof of Need

Ethic 1

Truth belongs to those experiencing the problem.

Top-down models cannot define the needs of sex workers, people who use drugs, LGBTQI+ communities, people living with HIV, or people in conflict zones.

Ethic 2

Need must be recognized without demanding vulnerability.

Evidence systems cannot punish people for telling the truth or force them to expose risky personal information.

Ethic 3

Need is not a competition.

Communities should never be placed in a position where they must exaggerate suffering to "win" funding.

Ethic 4

Need implies duty.

When Proof of Need is high, the system has a moral obligation to respond — not a discretionary choice.

Ethic 5

Need precedes impact.

We cannot measure "impact" without understanding "what needed to be impacted."

Cryptography and AI: Seeing Need Without Surveillance

The central tension: you must see need without exposing people.

Zero-Knowledge Proofs

Allow evidence of need without revealing identities

Differential Privacy

Protects small populations from identification

AI Aggregation

Aggregate patterns without collecting personal data

Community Oracles

Local validators confirm claims without exposing to outsiders

The system becomes transparent where it matters, private where it protects.
Proof of Need reconciles two old enemies: legitimacy and safety.

A Closing Thought

Traditional systems treat need as deficiency.
Proof of Need reframes it as signal — a message from the world about where life is becoming fragile, where rights are slipping, where resilience is thinning.

A dignified system listens to these signals.
A just system responds.
A wise system adapts.

Proof of Need is the beginning of such a system.
